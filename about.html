The Longer Version

I started trading forex at 18. Got wrecked. Learned. Kept going.

That experience taught me two things: (1) financial literacy is a privilege, not a default, and (2) the tools that exist for retail traders — especially in Africa and the Middle East — are garbage.

So I started building. First at fintechs in East Africa — Fingo Africa and others — shipping payment rails, card systems, KYC integrations, and AI support bots. I got obsessed with uptime (99.9%), with event-driven architectures that actually scale, with dashboards that tell the truth.

Now I'm building my own thing. Fifi Terminal is the market intelligence tool I wished existed — real-time data on African and MENA markets, news with sentiment analysis, AI research assistant. Zabili is prediction markets done right — Rust matching engine, on-chain settlement.

I also care about Islamic finance. Not as a niche, but as a lens: what would financial infrastructure look like if it was built from first principles around transparency, fairness, and no riba (interest)? That's what I'm exploring with Habib (halal investing + Zakat automation) and Golum Screener (Shariah + ESG screening for global equities).
